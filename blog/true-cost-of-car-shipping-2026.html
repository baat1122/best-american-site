The True Cost of Car Shipping in 2026

Real pricing data, hidden fee breakdowns, and a direct competitor comparison to help you understand the true cost of auto transport.

MR
Marcus Reid
Senior Logistics Coordinator · Neon Auto Transport · June 2026

Auto transport pricing can feel like a black box. Quotes for the exact same route can vary by hundreds of dollars, and many brokers advertise low "bait-and-switch" rates only to demand more money once your car is scheduled for pickup.

Understanding average market rates and how shipping rates are calculated is the best way to protect yourself from predatory pricing. Here is our comprehensive breakdown of the true cost of shipping a car in 2026.

Average Auto Transport Cost Ranges

Car shipping costs are primarily calculated per mile, but the cost per mile drops as the distance increases. Short-distance transport has a higher rate per mile due to the overhead of pickup and delivery.

  • Open Auto Transport: Typically averages $0.50 to $1.00 per mile. Coast-to-coast transport (e.g., California to New York) ranges from $700 to $1,500.
  • Enclosed Auto Transport: Costs about 30% to 40% more than open transport. Coast-to-coast rates range from $1,000 to $2,200 depending on the route and season.

5 Factors That Impact Your Quote

No two auto transport quotes are identical because pricing is highly dynamic. The five main factors affecting your rate include:

  1. Distance: Total miles is the main cost driver. Cross-country shipping has a lower rate-per-mile than regional shipping, but a higher total cost.
  2. Vehicle Size and Weight: Larger, heavier vehicles (like a Ford F-150 or a Chevy Suburban) take up more physical space on a trailer and consume more fuel, costing $100 to $250 more to ship than standard sedans.
  3. Seasonality: Car shipping rates peak during the summer months due to moving season and in the winter along "Snowbird routes" (e.g., New York to Florida).
  4. Transport Type: Open carriers are highly cost-effective; enclosed carriers offer premium protection at a higher rate.
  5. Pickup & Delivery Locations: Shipping between major metro areas close to major interstate highways (like I-95, I-10, or I-80) is cheaper than shipping to remote or rural areas, which require the carrier to detour.

Hidden Fees to Watch Out For

Many auto transport brokers use aggressive pricing models to secure bookings. They quote an artificially low price, collect an upfront deposit, and then tell you they can't find a carrier at that price. To avoid this, watch out for the following charges:

  • Upfront Deposits: Reputable brokers do not charge your credit card until a carrier is officially assigned and dispatched to pick up your vehicle.
  • Cancellation Fees: Avoid brokers who charge a fee if you cancel your order before a truck is dispatched.
  • Fuel Surcharges: Some brokers add variable fuel fees on top of the initial quote. Ensure your quote is all-inclusive.
  • Insurance Surcharges: Cargo insurance should always be included in your base quote. Never pay extra to ensure your vehicle is covered.
