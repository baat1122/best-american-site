How Much Does It Cost to Ship a Car Across the Country?

Complete 2026 cross-country car shipping cost guide with real per-mile rates, pricing factors, and route examples from Best American Auto Transport Inc.

MR
Marcus Reid
Senior Logistics Coordinator · Best American Auto Transport Inc · June 2026

The cost to ship a car across the country typically ranges from $900 to $1,800 using open auto transport, depending on the distance, vehicle size, transport type, pickup and delivery locations, time of year, and current carrier availability. Enclosed auto transport for the same cross-country distance costs between $1,200 and $2,500.

For example, shipping a standard sedan from the East Coast to the West Coast (approximately 2,800 miles) using open auto transport typically costs $1,100“$1,500. The same route using enclosed auto transport would cost $1,500“$2,100. Shipping a large SUV or truck adds $100“$250 to the base rate due to the extra weight and trailer space required.

7 Factors That Affect Cross-Country Car Shipping Costs

Understanding what drives your auto transport quote helps you budget accurately and identify fair pricing. These are the seven primary factors that determine how much it costs to ship a car across the country:

  1. Total Transport Distance: Distance is the single biggest cost driver. The further your car needs to travel, the higher the total price — though the per-mile rate drops for longer hauls. Cross-country routes like California to New York (2,800 miles) cost significantly more than regional moves like Virginia to Florida (800 miles).
  2. Vehicle Make, Model, and Dimensions: Larger and heavier vehicles — such as a Ford F-150, Chevrolet Tahoe, or Toyota Land Cruiser — take up more physical space on the carrier trailer and increase fuel consumption. Expect to pay $100“$250 more than the base sedan rate for SUVs, trucks, and oversized vehicles.
  3. Open vs. Enclosed Auto Transport: Open carrier transport is the most common and cost-effective option. Enclosed auto transport provides premium protection from weather, road debris, and UV exposure — recommended for luxury cars, classic vehicles, exotic automobiles, and high-value vehicles — at a 30–40% premium.
  4. Seasonal Demand and Fuel Prices: Car shipping rates peak during summer (June“August) due to moving season demand and along popular Snowbird routes in winter. Spring and fall typically offer the lowest rates. National fuel prices also directly impact carrier operating costs and are reflected in your quote.
  5. Vehicle Operability (Running or Non-Running): A non-running or inoperable vehicle requires special equipment (winch, forklift) and additional labor to load and unload. Most carriers charge a $100“$200 surcharge for inoperable vehicles. Vehicles that roll, steer, and brake normally are standard pricing.
  6. Pickup and Delivery Locations: Shipping between major metropolitan areas near interstate highways (I-95, I-10, I-80, I-40) is more affordable because carriers don't need to detour. Rural or remote locations require the driver to leave their primary route, adding fuel and time — which increases the cost by $100“$300.
  7. Expedited Shipping Requirements: Standard auto transport allows carriers to plan efficient multi-stop routes. If you need expedited or guaranteed pickup within 24“48 hours, expect a $200“$500 premium for the priority scheduling and dedicated routing.

Open vs. Enclosed Transport: Which Should You Choose?

Choosing between open auto transport and enclosed auto transport is one of the biggest decisions that affects your cross-country shipping cost:

Open Auto Transport

  • ✓ Most affordable option — 30–40% cheaper
  • ✓ Widest carrier availability nationwide
  • ✓ Safe for 95% of vehicles
  • ✓ Faster scheduling and pickup
  • — Exposed to weather and road debris

Best for: Daily drivers, standard vehicles, budget-conscious shipping

Enclosed Auto Transport

  • ✓ Complete protection from weather and debris
  • ✓ White-glove handling and loading
  • ✓ Hydraulic lift gates for low-clearance vehicles
  • ✓ Higher insurance coverage limits
  • — 30–40% higher cost

Best for: Luxury, classic, exotic, and high-value vehicles

How to Save Money on Cross-Country Car Shipping

Here are proven strategies to reduce your auto transport cost without sacrificing safety or reliability:

  • Book 2“3 weeks in advance: Last-minute bookings limit carrier options and drive up prices. Planning ahead gives carriers time to schedule your vehicle on an efficient route.
  • Be flexible with pickup and delivery dates: Allowing a 3“5 day pickup window lets the carrier consolidate loads, reducing cost.
  • Choose open transport for standard vehicles: Unless your vehicle requires enclosed protection, open carriers provide excellent safety at the lowest price.
  • Ship during off-peak seasons: Spring (March“May) and fall (September“November) offer the lowest rates due to reduced demand.
  • Pick up and deliver near major highways: If your address is in a rural area, consider meeting the carrier at a nearby city or interstate exit to avoid the rural surcharge.
  • Prepare your vehicle properly: Follow our vehicle preparation checklist to ensure smooth loading and avoid delays that could add fees.

How Long Does Cross-Country Car Shipping Take?

Transit time for cross-country car shipping depends on distance, carrier routing, and DOT hours-of-service regulations. Here are general guidelines:

  • Regional (under 500 miles): 1“3 days
  • Medium distance (500“1,500 miles): 3“7 days
  • Cross-country (1,500“2,500 miles): 5“9 days
  • Coast-to-coast (2,500+ miles): 7“14 days

These are estimates. Actual transit times depend on weather conditions, road construction, number of stops on the carrier's route, and whether you choose standard or expedited auto transport.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does it cost to ship a car across the country?

The cost to ship a car across the country typically ranges from $900 to $1,800 using open auto transport. Enclosed auto transport costs between $1,200 and $2,500 for the same cross-country distance. The final price depends on total distance, vehicle size and weight, transport type, pickup and delivery locations, time of year, vehicle operability, and current carrier availability.

How is car shipping cost calculated?

Car shipping cost is calculated based on the total transport distance, measured in miles. Auto transport companies charge a per-mile rate that decreases as distance increases. Short hauls under 500 miles average $1.20“$1.50 per mile, medium hauls of 500“1,500 miles average $0.85“$1.10 per mile, and cross-country hauls over 1,500 miles average $0.65“$0.85 per mile. Additional adjustments are made for vehicle size, transport type, and seasonal demand.

Is it cheaper to ship a car by open or enclosed transport?

Open auto transport is 30–40% cheaper than enclosed transport. Open carrier shipping for a cross-country move typically costs $900“$1,800, while enclosed transport for the same route costs $1,200“$2,500. Open transport is the most popular choice because it offers the best balance of affordability, safety, and carrier availability. Enclosed transport is recommended for classic cars, exotic vehicles, luxury automobiles, and high-value vehicles that need extra protection from weather and road debris.

What is the cheapest way to ship a car across the country?

The cheapest way to ship a car across the country is to use open auto transport with flexible pickup and delivery dates, ship between major metropolitan areas near interstate highways, avoid peak summer and holiday seasons, and book at least 2“3 weeks in advance. Choosing terminal-to-terminal shipping instead of door-to-door service can also reduce costs, though door-to-door remains the most convenient option.

How long does it take to ship a car across the country?

Cross-country car shipping typically takes 7 to 14 days from pickup to delivery, depending on the distance and route. For example, coast-to-coast transport (2,500“3,000 miles) usually takes 7“10 days. Regional transport (500“1,500 miles) takes 3“7 days. Transit time depends on carrier routing, number of stops, weather conditions, and DOT hours-of-service regulations.

Does car shipping cost more in summer?

Yes, car shipping costs are typically 10“20% higher during summer months (June through August) due to peak moving season demand. Winter shipping can also be more expensive on popular Snowbird routes from northern states to Florida, Texas, and Arizona. The cheapest time to ship a car is during spring (March“May) and fall (September“November) when demand is lower and carrier availability is higher.
