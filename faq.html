$ Cost & Pricing

Car shipping costs typically range from $500 to $1,500+ for cross-country transport. The final price depends on several key factors:

  • Distance — Price per mile decreases with longer trips (under 500 miles: $0.75–$1.00/mi; over 2,000 miles: $0.40–$0.60/mi)
  • Vehicle size — Larger vehicles (SUVs, trucks) cost more than sedans
  • Transport type — Enclosed transport costs 30–60% more than open transport
  • Season — Summer and winter are peak seasons with higher demand
  • Vehicle condition — Non-running vehicles may incur additional fees

Get an instant quote for your specific route and vehicle.

Auto transport pricing is market-driven and based on supply and demand along specific routes. Key pricing factors include:

  • Route popularity — High-traffic routes (e.g., California to Texas) tend to be more affordable due to carrier availability
  • Season and timing — Booking during off-peak seasons (fall, spring) saves money
  • Flexibility — Flexible pickup/delivery dates allow carriers to optimize routes, reducing costs
  • Vehicle dimensions — Height and weight affect how many vehicles fit on a carrier
  • Choose open transport (the industry standard and most affordable option)
  • Book 2–3 weeks in advance to avoid expedited shipping fees
  • Be flexible with pickup and delivery dates
  • Ship during off-peak seasons (fall and spring)
  • Remove unnecessary weight from the vehicle

Shipping Process

Transit times depend primarily on distance:

DistanceEstimated Transit
Under 500 miles1–3 days
500–1,500 miles3–5 days
1,500–2,500 miles5–7 days
2,500+ miles7–10 days

Pickup typically occurs within 1–5 business days of booking. Expedited shipping is available for faster timelines.

Follow this preparation checklist:

  1. Wash your vehicle — A clean car makes it easy to document existing condition with photos
  2. Remove personal belongings — Items are not covered by carrier insurance during transport
  3. Remove toll tags — Avoid unexpected toll charges during transit
  4. Check tire pressure and fluids — Tires should be properly inflated
  5. Leave gas at 1/4 tank — Enough to drive onto/off the carrier without excess weight
  6. Disable alarms — Prevent disruptions during transport
  7. Document condition — Note any existing damage on the Bill of Lading at pickup
  8. Remove aftermarket accessories — Secure or remove spoilers, roof racks, etc.

Most carriers allow up to 100 lbs of personal items stored in the trunk or cargo area. However, keep in mind:

Insurance & Safety

Yes. Every carrier in Best American Auto Transport Inc's network is thoroughly vetted and carries active cargo insurance and liability coverage as required by the FMCSA. Your vehicle is fully insured from the moment it is loaded onto the carrier until it is delivered at your destination. We verify all insurance certificates before dispatch.

All carriers carry cargo insurance required by the FMCSA. Here's the damage claim process:

  1. At pickup: You and the driver inspect the vehicle together and document its condition on the Bill of Lading
  2. At delivery: Compare the vehicle to the Bill of Lading documentation
  3. If damage is found: Note it on the delivery receipt and contact Best American Auto Transport Inc immediately
  4. Claim filing: We help you file a claim with the carrier's insurance company

Take photos at both pickup and delivery for the best documentation.
