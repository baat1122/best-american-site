Terminal-to-Terminal Shipping FAQs

What is terminal-to-terminal car shipping? +
Terminal-to-terminal car shipping is a cost-saving auto transport method where you drop off your vehicle at a designated terminal or hub near your origin, and pick it up at a terminal near your destination. This eliminates the need for a carrier to drive to your specific address, reducing costs significantly.
How much cheaper is terminal-to-terminal compared to door-to-door? +
Terminal-to-terminal shipping typically saves $100 to $300 compared to door-to-door service. The savings come from carriers being able to optimize their routes by loading and unloading multiple vehicles at a single hub location rather than making individual stops.
Where are the shipping terminals located? +
Shipping terminals are typically located in or near major metropolitan areas along interstate highways. Neon Auto Transport works with terminals across the country including in California, Texas, Florida, New York, Virginia, and other major shipping hubs. Contact us for the nearest terminal to your location.
Is terminal-to-terminal shipping safe for my vehicle? +
Yes. Terminal-to-terminal shipping uses the same fully insured, FMCSA-approved carriers as door-to-door service. Your vehicle is loaded, secured, and transported with the same care. Terminals are secure, fenced facilities where vehicles are stored safely until pickup.
How long can my car stay at a terminal? +
Most terminals allow vehicles to be stored for 1 to 5 business days after arrival at no extra charge. If you need more time, extended storage may be available for an additional daily fee. We recommend arranging pickup as soon as possible after your vehicle arrives.
TIPS & TRICKS

Tips for Terminal-to-Terminal Shipping

Know the Terminal Location

Ask your coordinator for the exact terminal address before drop-off day. Most terminals are located near major highways and interstates for easy carrier access.

Plan Your Transportation

You'll need a ride to the drop-off terminal and from the pickup terminal. Plan ahead with a friend, rideshare, or public transit to avoid last-minute stress.

Flexible Dates Save More

Combining terminal-to-terminal with flexible pickup and delivery dates can maximize your savings. Carriers prefer terminal hubs because they can consolidate loads efficiently.

Compare with Door-to-Door

Before choosing terminal-to-terminal, compare the savings against door-to-door pricing. For some routes, the convenience of door-to-door may be worth the small price difference.
